Infrared Spectroscopy in Criminal Investigations and Education

Chemistry Behind It

Molecules absorb specific IR frequencies that match their vibrational modes. Functional groups within molecules have characteristic absorption patterns that allow identification.

Applications

  • Forensic: Analyzing drug samples, paint chips, fibers
  • Educational: Teaching molecular vibrations and bonding
  • Industrial: Quality control in manufacturing

Benefits

  • Rapid, non-destructive analysis
  • Helps solve crimes through material evidence
  • Visual teaching tool for chemistry students

Safety Considerations

  • Requires proper handling of samples to avoid contamination
  • IR instruments must be calibrated regularly for accurate results
  • Some samples may require preparation with hazardous chemicals (e.g., KBr pellets)
