Nuclear Energy

Chemistry Behind It

Nuclear energy is produced through the fission of heavy elements such as uranium and plutonium.

Chain reactions release massive amounts of binding energy as nuclei split.

Einstein’s equation, E=mc², explains the conversion of mass into energy during these reactions.

Applications

  • Low-carbon electricity generation for power grids
  • Propulsion systems for naval ships and submarines
  • Research in nuclear physics and medical isotope production

Benefits

  • Low-carbon energy production helping combat climate change
  • High energy density for efficient long-term energy supply
  • Reliable economic base-load power generation

Safety Considerations

  • Long-term radioactive waste storage and containment
  • Strict plant safety systems to prevent accidents
  • Global efforts to control nuclear proliferation and weaponization
