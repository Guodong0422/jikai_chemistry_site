X-ray Crystallography

Chemistry Behind It

X-rays diffract from the electron clouds in crystalline materials, producing distinct diffraction patterns.

These patterns reveal atomic positions, allowing scientists to determine molecular structures.

The angles of diffraction are governed by Bragg's Law, which relates wavelength and crystal spacing.

Applications

  • Determining molecular structures (e.g., the DNA double helix)
  • Drug development by analyzing complex protein structures
  • Materials science research and development

Benefits

  • Provides unparalleled insights into molecular architecture
  • Accelerates drug discovery and materials innovation
  • Supports fundamental research in chemistry and biology

Safety Considerations

  • X-ray radiation requires effective shielding and safety barriers
  • Operators must receive proper training and certification
  • Access to equipment must be controlled to prevent accidental exposure
